Recipe #1

  • Time: 120min
  • Serves: smth
Ingredients Quantity
Rice 1 kg
Sheepmeat 1 kg
Carrot 1 kg
Onion 4 pieces
Garlic 2 pieces
Oil 300 ml
Baeberry 1 tbsp
Cumin 1 tbsp
Coriander 1 tbsp
Chill peper to taste
Salt to taste

Step 1: In a separate bowl, rinse the rice several times with cold water so that the last water is transparent.

Step 2: Rinse and dry the boneless lamb with a napkin. Then cut the meat into small cubes. Peel the onion and chop three onions into thin quarter rings. Chop the carrots into strips. Peel the garlic heads from the outer husk

Step 3: Pour vegetable oil into the cauldron and heat it so that a light smoke appears. Fry in oil until golden brown, a whole peeled onion, and then remove it.

Step 4: Now prepare a zirvak for Uzbek pilaf. Transfer the chopped onion into the heated oil and fry it while stirring for 5-7 minutes so that it turns dark golden in color, but does not burn.

Step 5: Then transfer the pieces of lamb to the fried onion and fry them for 10-15 minutes so that the pieces become ruddy on all sides. Fry everything over high heat.

Step 6:Next, transfer the chopped carrots to the cauldron and fry it for 3 minutes without stirring. After three minutes, mix all the ingredients with a spatula and cook the zirvak for another 10 minutes. Rub the coriander and cumin in your palms or in a mortar and pour this mixture into a cauldron. Add dried barberry and salt to your taste.

Step 7: Fry the meat with vegetables and spices until the carrots are soft. Then pour boiling water into the cauldron 2 cm above the meat level and simmer the zirvak already on low heat for 1 hour. You can add hot pepper to the zirvak according to your taste.

Step 8: After an hour, lay an even layer on top of the washed rice. Then increase the heat to maximum and pour boiling water through the skimmer or along the wall of the cauldron. The water level should be 3 cm above the rice layer.

Step 9: When the rice has absorbed all the liquid, press two garlic heads into it (only up to half), make the fire minimal and simmer the pilaf under a closed lid.

Step 10: After 10 minutes, tap on the wall of the cauldron with a slotted spoon, and if the sound is muffled, then in the pilaf make punctures to the bottom of the cauldron with a stick to remove steam through them.

Step 11: Then put a flat plate on top of the rice, and close the cauldron with a lid. Cook the pilaf for another 20-30 minutes on low heat. Then remove the garlic heads, mix the pilaf and remove the sample. Uzbek pilaf in a cauldron is ready. You can serve it to the table.

Have a good and delicious meal!
